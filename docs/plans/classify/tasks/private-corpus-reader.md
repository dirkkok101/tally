## Recipe

### Acceptance Checks

- The corpus path exists only inside structured input; the reader opens one regular owner-readable file read-only and refuses a final symbolic link, wrong owner, or permissive mode.
- Exact bytes are SHA-256 fingerprinted before rows stream in bounded order through source-generated JSON validation.
- Malformed, duplicate-ordinal, over-row, over-field, timeout, cancellation, and read failures emit stable metadata-only results and leave no copied or temporary corpus artifact.
- Tests prove paths, descriptions, normalized tokens, amounts, expected outcomes, and raw rows are absent from durable state and diagnostics.

### Failure Criteria

- Do NOT copy corpus data into classify.db, tracked fixtures, reports, logs, or graph entities - rejected by DD-CLASSIFY-PRIVATE-VALIDATION.
- Do NOT follow a final symbolic link or accept unsupported ownership or permissions.
- Do NOT call an external validation service or implement a second evaluator.
- Do NOT omit CancellationToken propagation across file and JSON streaming boundaries.

### Expected Outputs

- PrivateCorpusReader
- CorpusFingerprint
- PrivateCorpusRow boundary type

### Constraints

- The concrete reader is the one production implementation; no filesystem abstraction is introduced.

### Notes

- Private fixture payloads remain ignored and owner-only; only test generators and safe schemas are tracked.
