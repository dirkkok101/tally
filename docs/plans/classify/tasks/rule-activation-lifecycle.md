## Recipe

### Acceptance Checks

- Activation requires the exact current validation fingerprint, zero incorrect-application canaries, zero unexplained incompatible conflicts, an active category identity, and explicit owner actor and reason.
- One transaction creates the immutable RuleSetVersion, memberships, lifecycle event, and active pointer; any failure leaves the prior pointer unchanged.
- Broad apply is false by default and can be true only for the exact activated rule version and validation evidence explicitly authorized by the owner.
- Retire or validated replacement creates a successor set, retains prior versions, and records prior/resulting state plus replacement reference.
- Same-ID category rename refreshes display; archive, reactivation, identity change, stale evidence, or unavailable corpus blocks new authority.

### Failure Criteria

- Do NOT update a rule version or rule-set membership in place - rejected by DD-CLASSIFY-STATE-STORE.
- Do NOT activate automatically from save, validation, feedback, correction, import, startup, or evaluation.
- Do NOT grant broad apply when the corpus is missing or evidence is stale.
- Do NOT delete retired or superseded versions.

### Expected Outputs

- ActivateClassificationRuleCommand
- RetireClassificationRuleCommand
- RuleSetStore
- RuleLifecyclePolicy

### Constraints

- Rule-set activation and retirement are explicit SQLite transactions with attributable immutable history.

### Notes

- Replacement is activation of a validated successor that names its prior version; there is no separate update operation.
