## Recipe

### Acceptance Checks

- Validation binds candidate, corpus, expected-outcome, projection, category-lifecycle, and normalization fingerprints before processing.
- Every row is counted once; durable evidence contains aggregate suggestions, no-suggestions, conflicts, stale rows, canaries, and fingerprints only.
- Missing, stale, malformed, over-limit, incorrect-application, or unexplained-conflict evidence is non-activating and preserves the prior active rule set.
- An unavailable corpus blocks activation and broad authority but leaves evaluation by the unchanged active set available.
- Validation of 10000 rows enforces five seconds, 256 MiB, exact-limit acceptance, and one-over-limit rejection on linux-x64.

### Failure Criteria

- Do NOT create a separate validation evaluator - rejected by DD-CLASSIFY-PRIVATE-VALIDATION.
- Do NOT persist or export raw descriptions, normalized tokens, amounts, expected outcomes, rows, or source paths.
- Do NOT activate a rule or grant broad authority from validation.
- Do NOT treat missing corpus or stale fingerprints as a warning; fail closed.
