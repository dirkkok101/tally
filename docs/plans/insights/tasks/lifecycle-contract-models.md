## Recipe

### Acceptance Checks

- The contract assembly exposes closed enums and immutable records for ReportSnapshot, RestatementDifference, DeletionEvent, idempotency, backup, verification, restore, status, and PolicyValidationReceipt.
- ReportSnapshot stores the canonical report payload and provenance but never dated actual members, raw merchant descriptions, canonical transactions, or producer cursors.
- PolicyValidationReceipt contains only structural identifiers, fingerprints, aggregate outcomes, canary status, tool version, and owner acceptance fields and is explicitly external to insights.db.
- Snapshot, recovery, and replay identities are opaque stable values; collections have deterministic ordering and source-generated serialization produces stable schema fingerprints.
- Content fingerprints exclude derived reportId and contentFingerprint fields and distinguish every semantic lifecycle or recovery change.

### Failure Criteria

- Do NOT use string constants for system-owned states or statuses; use closed enums per ADR-CORE-0004.
- Do NOT add HTTP DTOs, frontend models, EF entities, policy scripts, arbitrary formulas, or caller-authored report payloads.
- Do NOT place dated actual members, raw merchant text, producer cursors, or private evaluation rows in snapshots, recovery receipts, status, or PolicyValidationReceipt.
- Do NOT include reportId or contentFingerprint in the semantic payload that is hashed.

### Expected Outputs

- Native-AOT-safe lifecycle and recovery contracts
- Lifecycle schema and privacy tests

### Constraints

- Use file-scoped namespaces, nullable annotations, immutable records, and no reflection-based serialization.
